My current workstation is an Asus Zenbook Duo, which I love, but it has one major flaw... it runs Windows. Unfortunately lighting design is not incredibly friendly to Linux as an industry.

I tried dual-booting and bricked my Windows install, which was terrifying. My Zenbook Duo suffers from poor Linux support because of the whole dual screen malarkey, and poor battery life partly because of the dual screen malarkey, but also partly because I killed it by keeping it plugged in with no charge limit set up for months at a time.

All that means that I'm in the market for a laptop which fits these minimum requirements:

* Quad-core processor
* 16GB RAM
* Upgradable SSD (preferably NVMe)
* Not completely god-awful screen (I used a T430 for five years with the stock panel. Never again)
* Thunderbolt support
* USB-C charging
* \>8h battery life
* Sub-£500 (~£350 optimal)
* Good Linux support

The idea is that this will become the computer I use for most tasks and the Zenbook becomes my CAD workstation I use for my lighting designs. They will probably both get similar amounts of use, but this laptop will be travelling with me *a lot* more than the Zenbook will. I'll be buying a used machine.
